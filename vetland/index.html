What is Histology?

Histology is the study of tissues at the microscopic level, revealing how animal bodies are constructed from cells. It's like being a tiny explorer in a cellular jungle! Essential for diagnosing diseases in vets by examining biopsies. üìñ

Key Concepts

  • Epithelial Tissues: Protective layers like skin. Types: simple, stratified. Helps with wound healing and barriers.
  • Connective Tissues: Bone, cartilage, blood. Provides structure‚Äîthink of it as the body's scaffolding for support.
  • Muscular Tissues: Skeletal (voluntary), cardiac (heart), smooth (organs). Powers movement and pumps blood.
  • Nervous Tissues: Neurons for signals. Crucial for reflexes and brain function in animals.

What is Physiology?

Physiology explores how organs and systems function to keep animals healthy. It's the "why" behind the "how" of life! Focuses on homeostasis and adaptations. üåü

Key Concepts

  • Cardiovascular System: Heart and blood vessels‚Äîpumps life like a cheerful drumbeat.
  • Respiratory System: Lungs and airways for breathing. Essential for those deep doggy sighs.
  • Endocrine System: Hormones regulating growth and metabolism. The body's tiny conductors.
  • Homeostasis: Maintaining balance, like keeping a cat's purr at the perfect temperature
